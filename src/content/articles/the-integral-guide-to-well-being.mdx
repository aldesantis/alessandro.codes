## Summary

Rest and restoration are essential for our well-being, allowing us to pause effort and replenish our energy. Taking time to rest helps us reconnect with ourselves and notice what truly nourishes us, rather than just what entertains us. In a fast-paced world, creating space for silence and non-stimulation is crucial for creativity and self-awareness.

## Highlights

- Rest isn’t just sleep, it’s giving ourselves permission to pause effort. To not force an answer. To not rush to fix, solve, change, or to become something other than what you are right now. It’s setting everything down, not engaging in any physical, mental, or emotional productivity.
- Notice what leaves you feeling more like yourself. Even if just a little.

## Metadata

Author: [[The Integral Guide to Well-Being]]

## Summary

The Integral Guide to Well-Being emphasizes that needs go beyond survival and include emotional and relational aspects essential for happiness and connection. Everyone has unique needs shaped by their experiences, and recognizing these needs can lead to personal growth and better relationships. Meeting basic needs, such as safety and love, is crucial for reducing harm and fostering a healthier society.

## Highlights

- Many of us struggle to articulate what our needs are. I believe this is in part because when our caregivers meet our needs, they also reveal them to us. For example, maybe we grew up in a home where all of our physical needs were taken care of, but our emotional needs largely weren’t. We may not even recognize we have emotional needs or what they are until well into adulthood because they were never revealed to us through care.

## Summary

Emotional regulation is the ability to understand and process our emotions without suppressing them. It helps us learn from our feelings and respond wisely, leading to a healthier and more fulfilled life. By finding a balance between overwhelm and numbness, we can meet our emotional needs with compassion and care.

## Highlights

- Our emotions have a lot of important information for us, we want feel them, or at least move in the direction of feeling them. So this is not about trying to make ourselves feel better, but _to get better at feeling_.
