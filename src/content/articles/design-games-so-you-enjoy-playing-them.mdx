## Highlights

- One of my metaphysical axioms is that existence is fundamentally playful.
- Playfulness is that which happens for its own sake. To inquire as to the purpose of play is to miss the point, and if you ask a child what they’re playing for you will get a confused expression. The point of play is play! What
- Navigating the world in a playful way doesn’t mean you can’t play towards a particular goal. It just means that the goal you want to achieve can’t be more important than _how_ you attain it.

- I also want to maintain a sufficient level of [not caring](https://www.michaelashcroft.org/notebook/not-caring-lets-us-perform-better) about whether or not I actually achieve my goals. It might happen, it might not, who cares, I’m just enjoying the ride. And by enjoying the ride it actually becomes more likely that I’ll keep doing the things that will allow my goals to ‘just be achieved’ one day.
