## Summary

OpenAI may be cosying up to vendors to get its ads business off the ground, but the real goal is for OpenAI to own the stack itself.

## Highlights

- The company is expected to burn through roughly $15 billion in cash this year, up from $9 billion in 2025, and has roughly 910 million weekly users – around 95% of whom don’t pay. Subscriptions alone won’t close that gap. Advertising is the most direct way to monetize the rest.
- For Criteo, the OpenAI deal arrives at a complicated moment. The company is staring down a $75 million revenue headwind in 2026 after two major retail media clients reduced their scope, and its stock was trading near 52-week lows last month.
- The irony is that The Trade Desk’s entire value proposition rests on being the open alternative to the walled garden and helping OpenAI scale its ads business could accelerate the arrival of exactly the closed, proprietary platform it has spent a decade positioning itself against.
