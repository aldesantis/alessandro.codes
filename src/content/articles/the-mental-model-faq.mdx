## Summary

Mental models are simplified tools that help us solve problems and make decisions. They often come from various disciplines, and understanding their limitations is important for effective use. Overall, mental models are useful because they reflect how humans think and learn.

## Highlights

- “The framework is not the thing. The thing (that lives in my head) is the thing. The framework is simply our best attempt at communicating the thing.”
- People want a key to success. They always have. In the distant past, it was the Puritan value system. Then it was business frameworks and magic quadrants. Mental models are just the latest flavour of that desire. Caveat emptor.
