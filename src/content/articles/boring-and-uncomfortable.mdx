## Summary

The author argues modern life removes useful discomfort and with it our ability to learn. He practiced “productive discomfort” by building small tools and rebuilding a failed supply chain. The lesson: design low-stakes, structured challenges to grow skills instead of buying convenience.

## Highlights

- Uncomfortable situations are usually the ones with the most potential for learning and innovation. If we continually avoid discomfort—and modern society increasingly pushes us to do exactly that—we lose our ability to do uncomfortable things.
