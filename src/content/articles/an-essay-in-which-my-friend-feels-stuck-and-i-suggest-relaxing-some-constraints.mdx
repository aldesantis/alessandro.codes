## Summary

The author discusses a conversation with a struggling artist friend who feels constrained by his definition of being a musician. He suggests that by relaxing these constraints and focusing on what truly makes him happy, like songwriting and performing, his friend can design a more fulfilling life. The key is to identify and challenge limiting beliefs to open up new possibilities.

## Highlights

- This makes me realize that what I said earlier in this essay is not enough. You can’t simply list what you want to do and figure out how to do more of that. You also need to surface the _unarticulated constraints_ that you have floating around, and that are making what you were born to do needlessly hard or impossible. You must bring into light the hidden thoughts that bind you.
