## Summary

The mixed response to luxury’s experiments with AI imagery is linked to a deeper question: Is the new technology expanding creative possibilities or undermining the sector’s promise of human artistry?

## Highlights

- AI resonates best in advertising when its use is transparent, aligned with a brand’s emotional and cultural codes and deployed to amplify rather than replace human creativity, said Prandelli. When those conditions are missing, even technically impressive campaigns can fall flat.
- Heritage maisons face a narrower path. Brands whose storytelling is anchored in craftsmanship and lineage need to be more careful, often using AI quietly as a tool rather than a theme, said Prandelli. When AI becomes too visible, it risks clashing with the semantic codes that underpin their authority.
