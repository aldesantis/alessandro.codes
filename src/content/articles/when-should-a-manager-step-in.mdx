## Summary

A manager should step in when a mistake could cause serious harm or when the team is stuck and needs guidance. They should support the team by giving clear vision and context while allowing autonomy. Good intervention helps the team learn and grow to handle decisions independently next time.

## Highlights

- So, when considering a team decision you disagree with, ask yourself: “if it’s a mistake, will lead to a hole ‘below the waterline’?”. If so, it’s a good sign you should intervene to save your team/employee from a fatal, potentially career-limiting mistake. The same goes with **one-way decisions** that are very costly to reverse.
- when you review your level of engagement, you should verify that it was appropriate for the individual’s particular TRM at the time.
