## Summary

“Reading occupies a strange position in today’s world, being at once physiologically unnecessary and culturally central.”

## Highlights
- *Language* is natural—we produce language automatically, as children, and we know of no human societies that lacked language—but *reading* is artificial. It had (and has) to be invented, like the crank.
