## Summary

Rebranding can help companies boost sales and attract new customers, but it often frustrates loyal fans. For a successful rebrand, brands should keep their original values and involve customers in the process. Timing and clear objectives are crucial to avoid alienating their existing fan base.

## Highlights

- “When you can identify what’s broken, then you know what to fix,” said Markman. However, rebranding is not a panacea for executive dysfunction, or a lack of market fit for products; Isken called these “Band-Aid rebrands,” when brands think that changing logos and packaging can fix an otherwise broken business model.
