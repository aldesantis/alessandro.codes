## Summary

New AI tools can create photorealistic digital twins so shoppers see clothes on versions of themselves. Retailers hope this boosts engagement and sales, but accuracy and emotional effects are uncertain. Brands must offer control, choice and fun to make the tech work for customers.

## Highlights

- Clayton’s research shows that [purchase intentions are greater when viewing ideal body types](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/03637751.2017.1332770), than those that are considered more realistic or plus sized. So while we might think we want to see ourselves accurately represented, we are more inclined to purchase if the image is idealised. “People want to purchase products that help them achieve their ideal selves,” she explains.
