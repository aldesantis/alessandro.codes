## Summary

Shein’s planned acquisition of Everlane shows how owning the entire supply chain can reshape fashion. This deal combines Shein’s fast, efficient production with Everlane’s focus on sustainability and transparency. Together, they may create a new model that meets customer demands for both speed and eco-friendly products.

## Highlights

- “They really have inverted the source of value,” says Thorbeck. “[Most] US brands think value is created on the front end of the relationship with consumers, through branding, merchandising, and style. But Shein turns that around by creating value with really rapid cycles, media trends, and the ability to respond and move on with far less risk… It really means that financial value is derived from the supply chain and through superior responsiveness to trends rather than frontend marketing and branding.”
