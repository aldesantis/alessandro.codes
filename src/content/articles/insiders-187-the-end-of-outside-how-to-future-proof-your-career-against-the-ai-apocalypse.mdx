## Summary

The article discusses how the rise of AI is changing the marketing landscape, making traditional concepts of “taste” irrelevant. It suggests that marketers need to adapt by leveraging AI and embracing new strategies to future-proof their careers. The focus should shift from seeking stable paths to navigating ambiguity and understanding evolving consumer behavior.

- Cultural products with mass recognition–especially over hundreds or thousands of years–are usually the product of all four modes of taste.
  Paintings with global recognition–the kind that is printed on cheap dorm room posters–were produced through communion with the divine, preserved and promoted as “art” worthy of study by _Secret Knowledge Cultivators_, and printed on posters and coffee mugs by _Pickers_ and _Scapegoats_.
- Before AI, the four dimensions of taste had a tangible benefit: they prevented brands and individuals from losing money.
  AI allows brands and individuals to bypass taste with no financial risk. You can test infinite iterations of an idea, then nuke the whole thing if it doesn’t work, and quickly move on to the next thing.
