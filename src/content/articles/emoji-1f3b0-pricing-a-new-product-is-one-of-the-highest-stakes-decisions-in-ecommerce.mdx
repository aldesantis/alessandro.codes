## Summary

‘Tis the season for new product lines and launches for certain categories, and we have been fielding a lot of questions from our customers on how to navigate this timely issue.

## Highlights

- Most brands reach for one of three standard approaches when pricing something new.
  **Cost-plus (most common):** Starting from cost of goods, adding your target margin, and calling it done. The math is clean, however this treats your internal economics as the pricing driver when customer perception of value is actually what determines whether someone buys. You might price at $45 for a healthy margin, but if customers see $65 worth of value there, you’ve left $20 on every sale.
  **Competitor Matching:** Using “market context” as the starting point has one problem, your product isn’t identical to theirs. When you anchor to a competitor’s price, you’re adopting their strategy without knowing whether they got it right. For genuinely differentiated products, there may not be a meaningful comparison at all.
  **Pre-launch Surveys:** This sounds scientific, however research published in the Journal of Marketing Research found that respondents are more price sensitive when real money is on the line versus when answering hypothetical questions. In plain terms: people overstate what they’d pay when no actual purchase is required. Survey data can inform your thinking, but it shouldn’t be the final word.
