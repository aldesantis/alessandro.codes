## Summary

People do not make decisions based purely on data because emotions are a crucial part of the process. Emotions help us choose by giving meaning and urgency that data alone cannot provide. Data supports decisions, but humans interpret it through feelings, experiences, and biases.

## Highlights

- One of the main ways we “corrupt” the purity of our data-driven decisions is by engaging in something called [motivated reasoning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motivated_reasoning).
  According to researcher [Ziva Kunda](https://fbaum.unc.edu/teaching/articles/Psych-Bulletin-1990-Kunda.pdf):
  “…motivation may affect reasoning through reliance on a biased set of cognitive processes—that is, strategies for accessing, constructing, and evaluating beliefs. The motivation to be accurate enhances use of those beliefs and strategies that are considered most appropriate, whereas the motivation to arrive at particular conclusions enhances use of those that are considered most likely to yield the desired conclusion.”
