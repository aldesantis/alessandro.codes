## Summary

Some people (especially great mathematicians) think and search for solutions wordlessly, using vague images, sensations, or patterns.
Wordless thought can be faster and richer but also more error-prone, while writing forces precision and reveals hidden gaps.
Practiced reading and writing build the mental structures that let the subconscious do deep, nonverbal thinking and make writing a useful tool for long chains of thought.

## Highlights

- So writing—and [reading, seriously, the writings of others](https://www.henrikkarlsson.xyz/p/how-i-read)—is a way to collect stepping stones: ideas that have been stabilized enough that they can carry us as we walk deeper into the thought space.
- At least my brain has the most miraculous autocomplete function and supplies me with credible endings to any sentence I start—often credible _nonsense_. But when the nonsense is there on the page, next to thoughts I’ve settled through hard work, it looks respectable! It often takes considerable work to realize I’ve fooled myself.
