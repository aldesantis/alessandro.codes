## Steps

1. Finely chop the onion, celery, and carrot.
2. Finely chop the fresh pancetta.
3. In a large pot, warm the oil, add the pancetta, and brown it over medium heat.
4. Add the chopped vegetables and let them soften gently without burning the onion.
5. Add the ground beef, mix well, and cook until it sizzles and dries out.
6. Deglaze with the red wine and let it evaporate completely.
7. Add the tomato paste, passata, and hot stock.
8. Simmer on low heat, covered, for about 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
9. Halfway through, adjust salt and pepper. The ragù should be thick and creamy.
10. Cook the spinach in a pan with a little water until wilted (5–6 minutes).
11. Drain, squeeze very well, and blend with the egg and yolks.
12. Make a well with the semolina and flour on a work surface and pour the spinach-egg mixture into the center.
13. Knead until you get a smooth, even dough.
14. Wrap and rest for 30 minutes at room temperature.
15. Roll the dough with a pasta machine to about 0.5 mm thickness.
16. Cut rectangles about 26 × 17 cm.
17. Blanch the sheets in lightly salted water for 30–40 seconds, then drain and dry on a clean towel.
18. Warm the milk without bringing it to a boil.
19. In a small pot, melt the butter, add the flour, and cook while stirring until you get a golden roux.
20. Pour in the hot milk a little at a time, whisking vigorously.
21. Season with salt and nutmeg.
22. Cook until you get a creamy but pourable béchamel.
23. Butter a 30 × 20 cm baking dish.
24. Spread a thin layer of béchamel and a thin layer of ragù on the bottom.
25. Add the first pasta sheet.
26. Continue with layers of béchamel, ragù, and grated Parmigiano.
27. Keep going until you have about 6 layers, finishing with ragù and Parmigiano.
28. Dot the top with a few small pieces of butter.
29. Bake in a preheated static oven at 170 °C for 40 minutes.
30. Let it rest a little before serving.
