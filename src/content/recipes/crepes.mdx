## Steps

1. Melt the butter in a small saucepan and let it cool slightly.
2. In a tall bowl, crack the eggs and whisk.
3. Add the milk and whisk until smooth.
4. Mix in the lukewarm melted butter.
5. Sift the flour into the bowl through a sieve and whisk vigorously until the batter is smooth and lump-free.
6. Cover with plastic wrap and rest in the fridge for at least 30 minutes.
7. Heat an 18 cm crêpe pan (or nonstick pan).
8. Grease with a small knob of butter.
9. Pour in a ladle of batter and spread evenly.
10. Cook for about 1 minute over medium-low heat.
11. Flip when the edges lift, and cook the other side for about 1 minute.
12. Repeat until the batter is used up.

## Notes

- The batter is neutral (no salt or sugar), so it works for both sweet and savory fillings.
- Butter in the batter is optional.
- Resting is essential to eliminate any lumps.
- You can use plant milk (rice, soy, oat) with the same ratios.
- Store the batter for up to 12 hours in the fridge, covered.
- Store cooked crêpes for 2 days in the fridge, covered.
- You can freeze crêpes separated by parchment paper.
