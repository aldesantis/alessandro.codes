## Ingredients

For the batter:

- Ripe banana flesh (450 g, about 4 bananas)
- All-purpose flour (00) (200 g)
- Softened butter (120 g)
- Sugar (120 g)
- Eggs (110 g, 2 medium)
- Baking powder (6 g)
- Chocolate chips (to taste)
- Fine salt (1 pinch)
- Lemon juice (½ lemon)
For the pan:
- Butter (as needed)
- All-purpose flour (00) (as needed)

## Steps

1. Squeeze the juice of half a lemon.
2. Peel the bananas, slice them into rounds, and place in a bowl.
3. Add the lemon juice to prevent browning, then mash with a fork until you get a smooth purée.
4. In another bowl, beat the softened butter (cut into pieces) with the sugar using an electric mixer on medium speed until smooth and creamy.
5. Add the eggs one at a time, keeping the mixer running so they incorporate well.
6. Mix in the baking powder and the pinch of salt, continuing to beat.
7. Fold the banana purée into the mixture with a spatula.
8. Add the chocolate chips, then sift the flour directly into the bowl.
9. Mix with a hand whisk just until smooth and lump-free, without overworking.
10. Transfer to a loaf pan (22.5×8.5 cm) buttered and floured. Smooth the top with a spatula.
11. Make a lengthwise cut with a knife blade dipped in melted butter to help the loaf split neatly down the center during baking.
12. Bake in a preheated static (conventional) oven at 180°C for about 60 minutes. Check doneness with a toothpick.
13. Remove from the oven and let the banana bread cool in the pan before unmolding and slicing.
