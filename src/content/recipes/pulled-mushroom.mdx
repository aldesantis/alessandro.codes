## Ingredients

- Oyster mushrooms (pleurotus) (500 g)
- Soy sauce (1 small espresso cup)
- BBQ sauce (30 ml)
- Rice vinegar (15 ml)
- Vegetarian oyster sauce (15 ml)
- Sesame oil (15 ml)
- Sugar (5 ml)
- Garlic clove (1)
- Salt (to taste)
- Extra-virgin olive oil (as needed)

## Steps

1. Remove any dirt and tough stem parts from the mushrooms. Rinse quickly under cold water (don’t soak).
2. Pull the mushrooms into small strips by hand or cut with a knife.
3. Put the mushrooms in a baking dish, drizzle with olive oil, and toss to coat.
4. Bake at 200 °C for 20–25 minutes, until dry and crisp.
5. Mix soy sauce, vegetarian oyster sauce, BBQ sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and sesame oil.
6. In a pan, warm a little oil with the smashed garlic clove. Let it infuse over low heat, then add the mushrooms and briefly sauté.
7. Pour in the sauce, toss, and let it thicken slightly.
8. Add about 120 ml of water to deglaze, then cook off until it clings to the mushrooms.

## Notes

- Use the pulled mushrooms to fill buns, wraps, tortillas, or steamed bao. Great hot or at room temperature.
