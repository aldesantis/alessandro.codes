## Ingredients

- Chicken breast (1 kg)
For the marinade:
- Greek yogurt (150 g)
- Fresh ginger (30 g, grated)
- Garam masala (5 g)
- Smoked paprika (3 g)
- Garlic cloves (3)
- Lime juice (½ lime)
- Fine salt (1 pinch)
For the sauce:
- Tomato passata (350 g)
- Fresh liquid cream (50 g)
- White onion (1)
- Garlic clove (1)
- Fresh chili pepper (1)
- Cumin (3 g)
- Smoked paprika (3 g)
- Ground turmeric (3 g)
- Brown sugar (20 g)
- Extra virgin olive oil (10 g)
- Fine salt (to taste)
- Fresh coriander or parsley (to taste)

## Steps

1. Cut the chicken breast in half lengthwise and remove any fatty parts.
2. Remove the central bone, if present.
3. Cut first into slices and then into cubes.
4. In a large bowl, mix the Greek yogurt, grated fresh ginger, garam masala, paprika, lime juice, and a pinch of salt.
5. Add the chicken pieces.
6. Crush 3 garlic cloves directly into the bowl.
7. Mix well until the meat is evenly coated.
8. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight (or at least 6 hours).
9. Remove the chicken from the fridge and let it come back to room temperature.
10. Transfer to a baking tray.
11. Cook in a preheated fan oven at 220 °C for 20 minutes, or until a golden crust forms.
12. Slice the garlic and onion thinly.
13. Cut the chili in half, remove the seeds, and chop into small pieces.
14. In a wide pan, add the oil and sauté the garlic, onion, and chili for 3 minutes.
15. Add the cumin, turmeric, and paprika, and toast the spices for one minute over medium-high heat.
16. Pour in the tomato passata.
17. Add the brown sugar and a pinch of salt.
18. Cover with a lid and cook over medium heat for about 10 minutes.
19. When there are about 2 minutes left, add the cream to the sauce and stir well.
20. Add the cooked chicken to the sauce in the pan.
21. Stir well to combine.
22. Add fresh coriander or parsley to taste.
23. Serve.

## Notes

- Store in the fridge for one day in an airtight container. Freezing is not recommended.
- Serve with chapati bread, naan, or pilaf rice.
