## Steps

1. Peel and thinly slice the onion.
2. Trim the celery and slice into rounds.
3. Halve the olives and remove the pits.
4. Wash and dry the eggplant, trim, then cut into pieces about 2.5 cm.
5. Do the same with the tomatoes.
6. Toast the pine nuts in a dry pan for a few minutes until golden.
7. Heat olive oil in a high-sided pot and fry the eggplant in batches for a few minutes.
8. When golden, drain with a slotted spoon and place on paper towels. Set aside.
9. In a large pot, heat a generous amount of olive oil and sauté the onion until softened.
10. Add the celery and sauté well.
11. Add capers, olives, toasted pine nuts, and tomatoes.
12. Sauté briefly, then cover and simmer on low for 15–20 minutes.
13. Make the sweet-and-sour sauce by mixing vinegar, tomato paste, and sugar until smooth.
14. After 15–20 minutes, season with salt and pour the sauce into the pot.
15. Stir, raise the heat, and cook until the sharp vinegar smell cooks off.
16. Turn off the heat, add the fried eggplant, and add plenty of basil.
17. Mix well, transfer to a dish, and refrigerate.

## Notes

- Caponata is best served cold or at room temperature.
