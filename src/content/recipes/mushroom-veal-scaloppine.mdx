## Ingredients

- Veal escalopes (400 g, thinly sliced)
- Champignon mushrooms (500 g)
- Butter (100 g, divided into two 50 g portions)
- Dry white wine (40 g)
- All-purpose flour (40 g, for dredging)
- Fresh parsley (to taste)
- Fine salt (to taste)
- Black pepper (to taste)

## Steps

1. Using a small sharp knife, trim the earthy base of each mushroom stem and gently scrape away any remaining dirt.
2. Slice the mushrooms and set aside.
3. Melt 50 g of butter in a skillet over medium heat.
4. Add the sliced mushrooms, season with salt and pepper, and sauté for about 5 minutes until golden.
5. Remove from the pan and set aside.
6. Lightly coat each veal escalope in flour, shaking off any excess.
7. In the same pan, melt the remaining 50 g of butter.
8. Place the floured escalopes in the pan, season with salt and pepper, and brown for about 4 minutes per side until a nice golden crust forms.
9. Pour in the white wine and let the alcohol evaporate completely.
10. Add the cooked mushrooms back into the pan with the veal.
11. Toss everything together for a minute to let the flavours meld.
12. Turn off the heat, scatter fresh parsley over the top, and serve immediately while hot.

## Notes

- If the mushrooms are relatively clean, use a brush or dry cotton cloth instead of water — rinsing damages their texture.
- For a creamier sauce, blend half the cooked mushrooms and stir them back into the pan for a velvety coating.
