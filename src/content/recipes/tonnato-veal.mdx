## Steps

1. Make the tuna sauce — see [[tonnato-sauce|Tonnato sauce]].
2. Wash, peel and chop the carrot and celery into pieces.
3. Halve the onion, and peel the garlic clove (leave it whole).
4. Remove any cartilage or fatty sinew from the veal roast.
5. Place the meat in a large pot together with the chopped vegetables, garlic, bay leaf, cloves, and black peppercorns.
6. Pour in the white wine and then enough water to cover everything.
7. Season with salt and add the olive oil. Bring to the boil.
8. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface. Cover with a lid, lower the heat slightly and cook for 40-45 minutes (allow approx. 30 minutes per 500 g of meat). The internal temperature of the meat should not exceed 65°C — use a kitchen thermometer to check.
9. Remove the meat from the pot and let it cool completely.
10. Strain and set aside 150 g of the cooking broth for the sauce; the rest can be used for risottos or other dishes.
11. Once the meat is completely cold, slice it thinly with a smooth-bladed knife.
12. Arrange the slices on a serving platter, spoon the tuna sauce generously over the centre, and decorate with whole and halved caper berries.
