## Steps

1. Pull the basil leaves from the stems, rinse them quickly under cold water, and dry them gently on a tea towel.
2. In a marble mortar, pound the garlic cloves (with the germ removed and cut in half) until they are reduced to a cream.
3. Add the pine nuts and continue pounding until you get a smooth paste.
4. Add the basil leaves and the coarse salt. Pound first with sharp strikes, then with rotating motions, occasionally gathering the mixture from the sides.
5. Incorporate the pecorino in small pieces and continue pounding. Add the Parmigiano as well and blend in.
6. Slowly drizzle in the extra virgin olive oil and pound briefly again until you get a smooth cream.

## Notes

- Use the pesto immediately or store it in the fridge, covered with a thin layer of oil, for up to 4 days. Alternatively, freeze it in small jars.
