## Steps

1. Melt the butter over low heat and let it cool slightly.
2. Blend the biscuits in a mixer until reduced to a fine powder.
3. Mix the biscuit powder with the melted butter in a bowl.
4. Butter and line a 20 cm springform tin with parchment paper.
5. Pour in the biscuit mixture and press firmly with a spoon to get a compact, even base.
6. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
7. Wash the mixed berries and pour them into a pan with the lemon juice and powdered sugar.
8. Cook over low heat for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
9. Transfer the fruit to a sieve set over a bowl to catch the juice — keep both aside separately.
10. Soak the gelatin sheets in cold water for 10 minutes.
11. Warm the cream in a small saucepan, then dissolve the well-squeezed gelatin in it, whisking until fully dissolved.
12. Let cool.
13. Sieve the ricotta through a strainer (if it has a lot of whey, let it drain for 1–2 hours).
14. In a bowl, combine the ricotta, cream cheese, lemon zest, and powdered sugar.
15. Mix well, then add the now-lukewarm cream with gelatin and combine everything.
16. Take the tin out of the fridge.
17. Pour half of the cream onto the base and level it with a spatula.
18. Distribute the drained berries on top, then cover with the remaining cream and level again.
19. Refrigerate for 2 hours.
20. Take the reserved berry juice (about 280 g) and pour it into a saucepan.
21. Add the sieved cornstarch and whisk to avoid lumps.
22. Bring to the heat and thicken over low heat for 5 minutes, then let cool, stirring often.
23. Pour the berry sauce over the cheesecake and level with a spatula.
24. Return to the fridge for at least 1 hour.
25. Garnish with fresh mixed berries and mint leaves.
26. Serve.
