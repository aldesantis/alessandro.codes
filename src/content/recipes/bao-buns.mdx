## Ingredients

- All-purpose flour (500 g)
- Fresh baker’s yeast (15 g)
- Sugar (5 ml)
- Water (300 ml)
- Extra virgin olive oil (15 ml)
- Fine salt (5 ml)

## Steps

1. In a large bowl, add the flour.
2. Add the sugar and the oil, and start mixing with a fork to incorporate the ingredients.
3. Mix the yeast and salt into the water.
4. Pour into the flour, continuing to mix first with the fork and then with your hands.
5. Transfer the dough to a work surface and knead by hand until you obtain a smooth, homogeneous dough shaped into a ball.
6. Cover the dough with an inverted bowl and let it rest until doubled in volume, about 1 hour.
7. After rising, work the dough into a log and divide it into eight equal pieces.
8. Shape each piece into a small ball.
9. Roll each ball out with a rolling pin into an oval shape.
10. Brush the surface of each oval lightly with oil.
11. Fold each oval in half to obtain the typical bao bun shape, then let them rest for about 15 minutes.
12. Meanwhile, bring water to a boil in a pot fitted with a steamer. Line the steamer basket with parchment paper.
13. Arrange the bao buns in the steamer, keeping them spaced apart.
14. Cover with a lid and steam for about 15 minutes, until they are puffed up and soft.
15. Serve as desired.
