## Steps

1. If using pre-soaked stockfish, rinse it thoroughly several times under cold running water.
2. Using tweezers, remove all the bones — this is easiest to do while the fish is still raw.
3. Cut the stockfish into large pieces.
4. Place the pieces in a tall, preferably stainless steel pot.
5. Add the bay leaves and cover with cold water.
6. Bring to the boil, then cook for a further 30 minutes. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface during cooking.
7. When the fish starts to flake apart easily, it is ready. Drain it well and transfer it to the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the whisk attachment.
8. With the mixer running at medium-low speed, pour in the sunflower oil in a thin, steady stream — much like making a mayonnaise.
9. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
10. Increase the speed and continue whipping for about 10 minutes in total, until you obtain a smooth, pale, creamy spread.
11. Using two spoons, form quenelles and place them on slices of toasted bread or grilled polenta.
12. Finish with freshly chopped parsley and a grind of black pepper.

## Notes

- If you don’t have a stand mixer, electric hand beaters work well. Whipping by hand would take considerably longer and much more effort.
- Pour the oil gradually and adjust the quantity based on the consistency of your fish — some batches absorb more oil than others.
- The cream will seem very soft right after whipping, but it will firm up as it rests.
- Some home versions include about 150 ml of milk added during cooking, which makes the whipping process easier.
