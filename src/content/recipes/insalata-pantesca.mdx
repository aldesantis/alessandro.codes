A traditional salad from the island of Pantelleria: boiled potatoes and cherry tomatoes with sweet red onion, oregano, olives, and the island’s prized salt-packed capers, dressed with extra virgin olive oil.

## Steps

1. Wash the potatoes and boil them whole, unpeeled, in cold salted water for about 30–35 minutes, depending on size.
2. Peel and thinly slice the red onion. Wash the cherry tomatoes and cut them in half.
3. In a large bowl, combine the onion, tomatoes, olives, and the desalted capers.
4. Season with the basil leaves torn by hand and the dried oregano.
5. Once the potatoes are done, drain, peel, and let them cool, then cut into chunks. Add them to the rest of the ingredients.
6. Dress with the white wine vinegar, olive oil, and salt to taste. Toss to combine and serve.

## Notes

- Keeps in the fridge for a couple of days; freezing is not recommended.
- Can be enriched with roasted flaked fish, boiled octopus, tuna or mackerel (plain or in oil), or shredded boiled chicken to turn it into a main dish.
