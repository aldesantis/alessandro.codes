## Ingredients

- Spaghetti (320 g)
- King prawns (900 g, shell-on)
- Cherry tomatoes (180 g)
- Dry white wine (50 g)
- Garlic (1 clove, unpeeled)
- Lemon (1)
- Chili pepper
- Fresh parsley
- Extra virgin olive oil
- Fine salt
- White pepper

For the bisque:

- Water (1 lt)
- Shallots (2)
- Dry white wine (50 g)
- White peppercorns
- Cherry tomatoes (100 g)
- Fresh parsley (1 sprig)
- Black peppercorns
- Extra virgin olive oil

## Steps

1. Clean the king prawns: remove the heads, legs, and shells. Run a knife along the back of each prawn and remove the intestinal vein. Set the shells and heads aside for the bisque.
2. Cut the cherry tomatoes into quarters.
3. Coarsely chop the shallots.
4. Heat a drizzle of 1 extra virgin olive oil in a large saucepan over high heat, add the shallots and the reserved prawn shells and heads.
5. Sauté, stirring and pressing the shells down with a wooden spoon to release their flavour.
6. Deglaze with the white wine, then add the black peppercorns, white peppercorns, fresh parsley sprig, and the halved cherry tomatoes.
7. Cover with water and cook over medium heat for about 30 minutes, skimming off any foam that forms on the surface.
8. About 20 minutes into the bisque cooking time, heat some extra virgin olive oil in a large frying pan with the fresh chili pepper and the garlic clove, unpeeled. Let them infuse for a couple of minutes.
9. Add the prawns and sear for 1–2 minutes, then deglaze with white wine and cook until the wine has completely evaporated.
10. Remove the garlic clove and lift the prawns out of the pan, leaving all the cooking juices behind. Cover the prawns and set aside.
11. Add the quartered cherry tomatoes to the same pan and cook for about 5 minutes.
12. Bring a pot of water to a boil, salt it, and cook the spaghetti for only half its indicated cooking time—it will finish cooking in the pan.
13. Strain the bisque through a fine sieve.
14. Drain the pasta while still very al dente and transfer it to the frying pan with the tomatoes.
15. Add the strained bisque a ladleful at a time, cooking it like a risotto and stirring frequently, until the pasta is perfectly cooked and the sauce is creamy.
16. Add the prawns back to the pan. Season with fine salt and white pepper. Finish with freshly chopped parsley and lemon zest. Serve immediately.

## Notes

- You can substitute spaghetti with linguine or spaghettoni. If you prefer, skip the lemon zest, or use passata instead of fresh cherry tomatoes. Omit the chili if serving to children. Best eaten right away.
