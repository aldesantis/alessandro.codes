## Ingredients

- Spaghetti (320 g)
- Zucchini (700 g)
- Provolone del Monaco (150 g, coarsely grated)
- Grated Parmigiano Reggiano DOP (30 g)
- Garlic clove (1)
- Extra virgin olive oil (30 g, for sautéing)
- Extra virgin olive oil (as needed, for frying the zucchini)
- Fresh basil (to taste)
- Fine salt (to taste)
- Black pepper (to taste)

## Steps

1. Wash and trim the zucchini, then slice them into thin rounds with a mandolin.
2. Heat plenty of extra virgin olive oil to about 150 °C and fry a few rounds at a time for 5–6 minutes, until golden.
3. Drain on paper towels and immediately season with salt and torn basil.
4. Coarsely grate the Provolone del Monaco and the Parmigiano.
5. Bring a pot with plenty of salted water to a boil for the spaghetti.
6. Cook the spaghetti and drain them very al dente (after about 6 minutes of cooking), reserving the pasta water.
7. In a wide pan, heat 30 g of extra virgin olive oil, add the unpeeled garlic clove, and let it flavor the oil.
8. Remove the garlic and add the fried zucchini.
9. Sauté the zucchini with a ladle of pasta water to make them soft and not dry.
10. Transfer the spaghetti to the pan with the zucchini and finish cooking risotto-style, adding pasta water a little at a time for up to 3 minutes.
11. Off the heat, stir in part of the Provolone to form a cream, then add the Parmigiano and the remaining Provolone.
12. Add torn basil, another ladle of pasta water if needed, and a grind of black pepper.
13. Serve immediately, very hot.
