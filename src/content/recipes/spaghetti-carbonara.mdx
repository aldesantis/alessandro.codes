## Ingredients

- Egg yolks (4)
- Whole eggs (2)
- Parmigiano Reggiano (180 g, 36 months)
- Pecorino romano (120 g)
- Guanciale or pancetta (as needed)
- Spaghetti (as needed)
- Salt (as needed)
- Black pepper (to taste, Tellicherry / Lungo del Bengala / your choice)

## Steps

1. In a heatproof bowl, add 2 whole eggs and 4 yolks.
2. Whisk vigorously to partially denature the proteins.
3. Add, per person, 20 g pecorino and 30 g Parmigiano (50 g total per person) and keep whisking.
4. Set the bowl over a bain-marie at about 90 °C (water simmering, not boiling hard).
5. Keep whisking while the egg white proteins start to coagulate and the cheese fats melt, making the cream fluid again.
6. Warm the zabaglione up to 62.5 °C, without going over 63 °C.
7. Once it goes above 62 °C, remove from the heat and bring it back under 60 °C while whisking.
8. Repeat this hot/cool cycle 5 times.
9. Cool it quickly by placing the bowl in ice water to stop coagulation.
10. Cut the guanciale (or pancetta) and cook it in a pan until crispy.
11. Cook the spaghetti in salted water.
12. Meanwhile, heat a pan (not warm: it should be properly hot).
13. Drain the spaghetti directly into the pan, add 30 ml pasta water, and toss vigorously to release starch and create a creamy base.
14. Turn off the heat.
15. Add 30 ml savory zabaglione and 15 ml guanciale per serving.
16. Mix until you get the texture you want.
17. Twist the spaghetti onto plates.
18. Add extra crispy guanciale.
19. Finish with the pepper you chose (Tellicherry, Lungo del Bengala, etc.).

## Notes

- Final ratio per serving: 30 ml savory zabaglione and 15 ml crispy guanciale per plate.
