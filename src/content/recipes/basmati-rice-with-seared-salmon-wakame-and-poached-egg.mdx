## Ingredients

- Basmati rice (160 g)
- Fresh skinless salmon fillet (200 g)
- Very fresh eggs (2)
- Dried wakame (15 ml)
- Light soy sauce (15 ml)
- Mirin (5 ml, or dry white wine)
- Toasted sesame oil (5 ml)
- Rice vinegar (5 ml, or apple cider vinegar)
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Spring onion (thinly sliced)
- Fresh ginger (grated)
- Salt (to taste)
- Pepper (to taste)

## Steps

1. Rinse the basmati until the water runs clear.
2. Cook by absorption: 1 part rice to 1.5 parts water, plus a pinch of salt.
3. Rest covered, then fluff with a fork.
4. Soak the wakame in cold water for 10 minutes.
5. Squeeze dry and slice into strips.
6. Cut the salmon into 2 cm cubes.
7. Heat a pan with a drizzle of sesame oil.
8. Sear the salmon 1–2 minutes per side: browned outside, still tender inside.
9. Deglaze with mirin and soy sauce and let it evaporate.
10. Bring a pot of water to a boil with 5 ml vinegar.
11. Lower the heat so it just simmers.
12. Crack the egg into a small bowl, create a gentle whirlpool, and slide the egg in.
13. Cook 2.5–3 minutes: set white, runny yolk.
14. Lift out with a slotted spoon and blot on paper.
15. Divide the warm rice between two bowls.
16. Top with salmon, wakame, and a poached egg.
17. Finish with sesame seeds, spring onion, and grated ginger.

## Notes

- Optional: finish with a drizzle of sesame oil or a few drops of ponzu.
