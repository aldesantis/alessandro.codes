When you are just starting out in your career, you ask dumb questions, mostly about the mechanics of the work that you’re doing. You’re too busy building the [[learning-is-a-four-phase-process|right intuition]], so people only give you easy, well-defined problems to tackle.

As you progress in your career, however, you start asking more and smarter questions. This is when people start giving you ill-defined problems, where you may not have all the tools to solve the problem right away, but everyone around you trusts you to figure things out.
